Clinical Alert · Toxicology

Cannabinoid Hyperemesis Syndrome: 500%+ Surge Since 2016 — What's Driving It

Emergency departments nationally are reporting a dramatic rise in marijuana-induced illness per ACEP Now July 2026, with cannabinoid hyperemesis syndrome (CHS) up more than 500% since 2016. The driver: dramatically increased potency in commercial cannabis products. CHS presents cyclically — prodromal phase (weeks of nausea), hyperemesis phase (intractable vomiting), then recovery. The compulsive hot-shower behavior remains the most distinctive diagnostic clue. Standard antiemetics (ondansetron, promethazine) are often ineffective. First-line antiemetics with better evidence: haloperidol and droperidol. Topical capsaicin cream (applied to abdomen) has emerging supportive data. Worth an updated tox teaching block for residents.
