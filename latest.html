Top Highlight · Jun 17, 2026
Overdose Deaths Post Steepest Sustained Drop on Record

The CDC's June 17 provisional release projects 69,147 U.S. drug overdose deaths for the 12 months ending January 2026 — a 13.2% year-over-year decline and, per Medical Daily, the most dramatic, sustained drop in overdose mortality ever recorded.

Researchers point to shifting fentanyl potency and supply patterns rather than a single intervention. Still tens of thousands of preventable deaths a year — and a reminder that supply-side shifts can move faster than treatment infrastructure.

Journal · Annals of EM
When Triage Misses, Outcomes Suffer

A retrospective cohort across 21 EDs (2016–2020) by Sax, Reed, and colleagues in Annals of Emergency Medicine compared assigned Emergency Severity Index against downstream resource use.

Both undertriage of high-acuity patients and overtriage of low-acuity patients were linked to worse timeliness of care and outcomes — worth raising at your next triage-protocol committee meeting.

Toxicology Alert
The Animal Tranquilizer Naloxone Can't Touch

Medetomidine — a veterinary alpha-2 agonist — is spreading through the illicit fentanyl supply, per a new review by Lynch, Pizon, and Yealy in Annals of Emergency Medicine. It causes prolonged sedation, bradycardia, and biphasic blood pressure swings that persist after naloxone.

It also triggers a withdrawal syndrome that's faster, more severe, and less naloxone-responsive than typical opioid withdrawal — with no approved reversal agent. Worth a department-wide heads-up if you haven't seen a case yet.

Healthcare Technology · Dated Jun 19, 2026
AI Cuts OR Scheduling Error in Half at Mayo Clinic

Why it matters for a physician-entrepreneur: this is a clean before/after efficiency number from a name-brand health system — exactly the kind of evidence that gets hospital administrators to say yes to a new tool.

Opmed and Mayo Clinic reported that a multimodal AI platform combining structured clinical data with unstructured preoperative notes cut cardiovascular procedure scheduling error from a baseline 1.13-hour mean absolute error down to 0.564 hours, evaluated across 643 cardiovascular procedures from November 2025 to January 2026.

The model doesn't replace the scheduler — it flags cases likely to run long or short before they hit the board. Same underlying logic could eventually extend to ED boarding and throughput prediction, a problem every academic ED chief lies awake thinking about.

Is Primary Care a Right or a Commodity?

A NEJM Perspective argues primary care is drifting from a common-good model toward a free-market commodity — accessible based on ability to pay rather than need — as DPC and concierge practices expand precisely because traditional reimbursement no longer covers the cost of relationship-based care.

Useful ammunition for either side of the free-market-medicine debate, depending which side you're arguing.

DPC Tops 2,600 Practices as HSA Rules Loosen

The U.S. now counts roughly 2,601 indexed concierge and DPC practices, with practice sites up more than 80% since 2018.

The tailwind: the Primary Care Enhancement Act, effective January 1, 2026, lets patients pay DPC membership fees (up to $150/month individual, $300/month family) with pre-tax HSA dollars — removing the audit-risk ambiguity that kept some HSA-eligible patients on the sidelines.

Legislation
A Tax Parity Bill for Health Sharing Members

Members of the House Ways & Means Health Subcommittee introduced the Health Care Sharing Ministry Tax Parity Act, which would let HCSM members deduct their monthly shares as qualified medical expenses — the same tax treatment traditional insurance premiums already get.

It doesn't reclassify sharing as insurance; it just closes a tax gap members have flagged for years.

Rep. Mike Kelly Press Release

IRS Guidance
HRAs May Soon Reimburse Health-Share Payments

A proposed IRS rule would let employer-funded HRAs reimburse employees tax-free for HCSM shares, DPC membership fees, and certain public-coverage payments — without legally calling any of them "insurance."

For the roughly one million Americans on health-sharing plans, it's a meaningful crack in a tax code that's long treated shares as second-class to premiums.

New Income Idea · Finance
Sell Covered Calls Against the MSTR You Already Own

This is a new income stream, not a new investment: against MSTR shares already held, sell out-of-the-money covered calls expiring 30–45 days out. MSTR's implied volatility is elevated after this month's drawdown, meaning option premiums are richer than usual right now.

Starting point: enable options trading (Level 1, covered calls only) on an existing Fidelity or Schwab brokerage account — no new account needed — then sell one contract per 100 shares held. Realistic range: roughly 1–3% of position value per month in premium ($500–$2,000+/month depending on share count). Trade-off: upside above the strike is capped if the stock rallies hard. Friction: requires options-trading approval (usually fast for covered calls) and willingness to have shares called away.
