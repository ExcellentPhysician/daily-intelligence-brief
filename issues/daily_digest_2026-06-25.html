ACEP Praises Final IDR Rule Strengthening Out-of-Network Pay Protections

The Independent Dispute Resolution Operations final rule, released May 28, tightens the No Surprises Act's arbitration process — something ACEP has pushed for as EM groups absorb low or delayed out-of-network payments.

The rule is meant to speed dispute resolution and produce more consistent, fair reimbursement outcomes for physicians billing out of network — directly relevant to any EM group's revenue cycle.

Does ED Triage Accuracy Actually Change Outcomes?

A new Annals of Emergency Medicine study, in press this June, examines the association between ED undertriage or overtriage and both timeliness of care and patient outcomes.

It's a direct look at whether getting the acuity score "wrong" at the front door measurably hurts patients downstream — relevant reading for anyone shaping triage protocols or resident education on acuity scoring.

Source: Annals of Emergency Medicine Read More

Bonus Item · Resuscitation Debate
Is Epinephrine Doing More Harm Than Good in Cardiac Arrest?

Writing for Medscape this month, Drs. Robert Glatter and Peter Antevy argue that epinephrine may worsen outcomes in shockable cardiac arrest, and that early defibrillation, double sequential external defibrillation (DSED), and high-performance CPR should take priority over the drug.

A useful one to revisit before the next code committee meeting or resident resuscitation talk.

DPC · 2026 Rule Change
DPC Fees Are Now HSA-Eligible

As of January 1, 2026, patients enrolled in qualifying direct primary care arrangements — generally those charging under $150/month and staffed by family medicine, internal medicine, geriatric, or pediatric-trained physicians — can contribute to an HSA and pay their DPC membership tax-free from it.

It removes a years-long compliance gray zone tracked closely by DPC Frontier — a meaningful tailwind for the model's growth case this year.

Source: DPC Frontier Read More

Federal Legislation
A Federal Tax Parity Bill for Health Sharing Members

Reps. Mike Kelly, Greg Murphy, and Chris Smith introduced the Health Care Sharing Ministry Tax Parity Act, which would let members deduct their HCSM membership payments by classifying them as eligible medical care expenses.

That's the same tax treatment traditional insurance premiums already receive — a real parity fix if it passes.

Source: Office of Rep. Mike Kelly Read More
📑
IRS Guidance · 2026
IRS Proposes Letting HRAs Reimburse Health Sharing Payments

A proposed IRS rule would let employer HRAs reimburse employees tax-free for monthly payments to health care sharing ministries, treating HCSM "shares" the same as qualified medical expenses, according to Take Command Health's breakdown.

Combined with the Tax Parity Act introduced this year, it's the clearest regulatory momentum HCSMs have had in years.
