No Surprises Act · May 28, 2026
Federal IDR Operations Final Rule Lands — ACEP Applauds

HHS, Labor, Treasury, and OPM finalized the Federal IDR Operations rule on May 28, cutting the per-dispute administrative fee from $115 to $15 — an 85%+ reduction, effective for disputes filed June 11 or later — and tightening the communication requirements that have plagued the No Surprises Act's dispute process since it launched.

ACEP, ACR, and ASA jointly applauded it as the most significant structural fix yet to a process that's been a real source of friction for out-of-network EM billing.

MetroHealth Steps Back From Pediatric Trauma Verification

On June 10, MetroHealth announced it will not seek ACS reverification of its Level II Pediatric Trauma Center designation, citing a regional decline in pediatric trauma volume and a nationwide shortage of pediatric neurosurgeons.

All four MetroHealth EDs (Main, Brecksville, Cleveland Heights, Parma) keep treating pediatric emergencies and will stabilize/transfer serious pediatric injuries to UH Rainbow Babies & Children's, while MetroHealth remains the region's Level I adult trauma center. A real regional trauma-system realignment with direct implications for ED disposition and transfer protocols.

DPC · HSA Policy, One Year In
The DPC-HSA Fix Didn't Fix Access

Direct primary care advocates are about to mark one year since federal law let HSA dollars pay DPC membership fees — but a KevinMD analysis published this month argues the bigger effect wasn't improved affordability.

IRS Notice 2026-05 caps the HSA-eligible membership fee at $150/month, so a practice pricing itself even slightly above that (say, $160/month) disqualifies its patients' HSA contributions for the year — an odd incentive nudging some practices to hold pricing down rather than expand capacity.

DPC · Market Structure · Jun 8, 2026
Mending Exits the Insurance Business to Go All-In on DPC

Mending told Maine's Bureau of Insurance on June 8 it's exiting its health-plan carrier business in Maine and Oklahoma by January 2027, affecting roughly 1,100 members — even as it doubles down on Mending Access, a platform launched earlier this year to help self-funded employers and TPAs bolt direct primary care onto their existing plans.

A small but telling data point on where the DPC-adjacent insurance market is placing its bets: away from being a carrier, toward being the DPC integration layer.

Where Health Sharing Actually Stands on Tax Deductibility

A 2026 HSA for America guide lays out the current (non-)deductibility of health sharing payments in plain terms: absent the Tax Parity Act passing, HCSM contributions still don't count as deductible medical expenses or qualify for HSA reimbursement the way insurance premiums or DPC fees under the $150/month cap now do.
