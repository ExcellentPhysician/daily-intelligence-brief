Top Highlight
No Surprises Act Dispute Process Gets a Real Fix

HHS, Labor, Treasury, and OPM released the final IDR Operations Rule on May 28, and ACEP, ACR, and ASA all applauded it. The administrative filing fee for the independent dispute resolution process drops from $115 to $15 per party, and insurers now must hand over all information needed to process claims correctly.

ACEP President L. Anthony Cirillo, MD called it "meaningful progress" toward a system that's "predictable, efficient, and grounded in transparency" — directly relevant to any out-of-network EM billing dispute.

"Bot vs. Bot": Why Hospital AI Keeps Hitting the Same Wall

At HFMA's 2026 annual conference, executives from Oracle Health, HCA Healthcare, and BJC Health argued — on the record — that no amount of sophisticated AI can overcome a broken, antiquated data foundation. The panel's blunt framing: health systems keep buying smarter models and layering them on top of the same fragmented, inconsistent data, which is why so much AI spend isn't translating into better care or lower cost.

Why it matters for a physician-entrepreneur: it's a useful gut-check before recommending or adopting any new clinical AI tool — the model is rarely the bottleneck; the data plumbing underneath it usually is. Covered by MedCity News.

Federal
Insurers Push Back on Medicaid Work-Requirement Timeline

CMS issued an interim final rule tightening the medical-frailty exemption tied to the new Medicaid work requirements (disability, substance use disorder, and complex medical conditions still qualify for exemption). The requirement itself — 80 hours/month of work for adults 19–64 — takes effect January 1, 2027.

At AHIP 2026, healthcare leaders publicly asked CMS for a state-implementation extension, per MedCity News: "you can't create systems in six months... I'd argue sometimes in even 18 months."

Ohio
Senate Greenlights Gig-Platform Staffing Bill

The Ohio Senate unanimously passed Senate Bill 423 this week, updating state labor law to cover healthcare workers booked through staffing apps that connect them with care facilities short-term. Sponsor Sen. Susan Manchester (R-Waynesfield) framed it as a tool for hospitals — including systems like MetroHealth — to fill gaps faster.

The bill arrives a month after the Ohio Nurses Association reported that over 91% of Ohio healthcare workers say their workplace isn't always appropriately staffed. SB 423 now moves to the Ohio House.

The DPC/HSA Fix Created a New Problem

KevinMD lays out the catch in this year's HSA rule for DPC: patients in qualifying high-deductible plans can now pay DPC membership fees with HSA dollars, but IRS Notice 2026-05 caps that at $150/month. Any DPC practice priced above that cap — common in higher cost-of-living markets — leaves HSA-paying patients losing their contribution eligibility for the year entirely.

A reminder that "free market" fixes still run through a regulatory needle's eye.
