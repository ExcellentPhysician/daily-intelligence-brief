Article Date: July 14, 2026
A Skin-Like Computer Patch That Runs AI Diagnostics On-Body, No Cloud Required

Researchers at the University of Chicago Pritzker School of Molecular Engineering built a stretchable, skin-like computing patch that runs AI inference directly on the body — no wireless transmission to a server — detecting life-threatening cardiac arrhythmias with 99.6% accuracy in milliseconds. Findings published in Nature Electronics.

Why it matters to you: this is the same latency-versus-cloud tradeoff you think about with POCUS at the bedside — on-device inference means a decision-relevant signal (arrhythmia, in this case) doesn't wait on a network round-trip. Lead researcher Sihong Wang frames the goal as "a personal, instantaneous doctor integrated into their devices." Early-stage, but it's a preview of where wearable-triage hardware is headed next for prehospital and ED-adjacent monitoring.
