ACGME Holds Off on EM Residency Length Changes

The ACGME announced it needs more information before altering the length of current EM residency programs, responding to a joint letter from ACEP and EMRA. For an academic attending training residents, this keeps the 3-vs-4-year debate open rather than settled — worth watching before curriculum planning assumptions get locked in.

Separately, EM's 2026 residency application cycle moved from ERAS to the new Residency CAS platform, aimed at lowering applicant cost and improving holistic review.

Skin Patch Detects Arrhythmias On-Device, No Cloud Required

Researchers at the University of Chicago developed a flexible, wearable computing patch that detects life-threatening arrhythmias at 99.6% accuracy — and processes the signal entirely on the body itself rather than streaming it to the cloud. That matters for an ED physician thinking about pre-hospital triage: near-real-time arrhythmia flagging without network dependency or the privacy exposure of continuous cloud transmission is a genuinely different design choice than most current wearables.

It lands alongside a broader July trend toward a "headless AI" coordination layer for connecting siloed hospital systems, and CMS's newly formed Office of Health Technology Products, which will shape how devices like this eventually get reimbursed.

A new IRS proposed rule would let Health Reimbursement Arrangements pay tax-free toward HCSM "shares," direct primary care fees, and some public coverage premiums — treating them as qualified medical expenses. Roughly a million Americans currently use health sharing programs.
