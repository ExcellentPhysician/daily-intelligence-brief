Ohio State Policy

SB 423: Ohio Senate Passes Healthcare Independent Contractor Bill

The Ohio Senate approved SB 423, codifying 15 criteria for classifying healthcare workers as independent contractors — specifically targeting platform-based (gig) healthcare staffing arrangements. The bill awaits Ohio House action.

Key criteria: formal agreements required, workers can reject shifts without penalty, no noncompete clauses permitted, platforms may not control the means and methods of work. Critics from the staffing industry argue the definition undermines patient care flexibility. For academic EPs considering locums or per diem work, this bill directly affects Ohio-based platform employment terms. The Health Policy Institute of Ohio projects Ohio families are paying ~$2,175 more in combined household costs since Jan 2025 due to federal policy changes.

IRS Proposes Rule Expanding HRA Reimbursement for Health Sharing Payments

A significant January 2026 IRS proposed rule would allow Health Reimbursement Arrangements (HRAs) to reimburse employees' monthly health sharing ministry payments tax-free. Crucially, the IRS is not classifying HCSMs as insurance — it's treating sharing payments as an eligible medical care expense under a separate pathway. This distinction matters: employers offering HRAs could now cover employee HCSM memberships without triggering ACA compliance issues. For self-employed physicians or DPC owners, the interaction with Solo 401k and HSA planning deserves attention. The rule is in proposed status; public comments were accepted through spring 2026.
