AI Drafts the Radiology Report Before the Radiologist Sees It

On June 25, Aidoc announced the FDA granted Breakthrough Device Designation to First Read, an AI that analyzes chest radiographs and auto-generates preliminary report text for four life-threatening findings — pneumothorax, free air, aortic dissection, and similar time-critical reads. It's investigational only for now, not yet cleared, but the designation fast-tracks FDA review.

The relevance to an academic ED is direct: a system that pre-drafts the radiology read on the studies most likely to kill someone in the next hour is squarely aimed at the gap between "image acquired" and "radiologist available," which is exactly where EM loses time. A related clearance landed the same week — GuideAI Health's VascularAssist Occlusion Triage got full FDA clearance for flagging suspected peripheral artery occlusions for prioritized review.

Medicaid Work Requirements Start Their Clock

CMS's June 1 interim final rule requires certain adult Medicaid enrollees to log 80 hours per month of work, school, or community engagement to keep eligibility, with states required to implement by January 1, 2027.

The same week, the House Energy & Commerce Oversight Subcommittee held a hearing on Medicaid program integrity, with state Medicaid directors — including Ohio's — testifying alongside New York, California, and Minnesota, per the National Law Review's federal healthcare update. Expect downstream ED visit volume effects as coverage churn plays out through next year.

Policy Friction
The DPC-HSA Cap Is Pricing Practices Into a Corner

A sharp KevinMD piece this month argues the IRS's $150-per-month HSA-compatibility cap for DPC memberships (Notice 2026-05) hasn't fixed access at all — practices pricing slightly above the cap, even at $160/month, cause patients to forfeit HSA contribution eligibility for the entire year, which is pushing some practices to either underprice their service or lose HSA-eligible patients outright.

A useful one to file away if you ever revisit a DPC-adjacent side venture — the cap is a real constraint, not a rounding error.

Independent Practices Lean Into DPC as a Reform Path

Medical Economics reports independent physicians are increasingly treating direct primary care and concierge models as a structural reform path rather than a niche option — with concierge/DPC practice sites up 83.1% and participating clinicians up 78.4% since 2018, even as overall primary-care access keeps tightening.

$1,000 Today
Don't Double Down — Top Off the HSA Instead

With the Fear & Greed Index at 17 and MSTR trading at a discount to its own BTC stash, the instinct might be to buy the dip. But you're already concentrated in crypto-correlated risk through MSTR and STRC preferred, on top of real estate exposure through Tabbut Real Estate LLC and irregular 1099 income. Adding more of the same risk isn't diversification — it's leverage on a thesis you've already taken.

The better use of $1,000 right now: push it into the 2026 family HSA, which carries an $8,750 cap and remains the only triple-tax-advantaged account available (deduction going in, tax-free growth, tax-free qualified withdrawals). If the HSA and Roth IRA ($7,500 limit for 2026) are already maxed for the year, then a small, direct BTC spot purchase — not more MSTR — is the cleaner way to add exposure without compounding equity-wrapper risk during a NAV-discount period.

Turn the Fountain Pen Habit Into a Content Channel

The pitch: a small, niche YouTube/affiliate channel reviewing fountain pens, inks, and paper — a genuinely non-medical income stream that draws on a collecting habit you already have, not a new skill you'd need to build.

Why it fits: the Goulet Pen Company has built a 25-million-view YouTube channel purely on educational pen content, proving there's a durable, low-competition audience for this; you already write at a Playfair-Display level of craft and have a camera and a backlog of pens.

Income range: realistically $50–$300/month in year one from YouTube ad revenue plus Amazon Associates links; established pen-community creators report $1,000–$3,000/month once a channel has scaled subscribers and a posting cadence.

Start today: register at Amazon Associates for pen/ink/paper links, then post one honest review video.

Friction: low cost (you own the inventory already), but real time cost per video for filming/editing, and a slow audience-building runway — this is a multi-month project before it's real money.
