Key points for the ED: (1) standard urine tox screens do NOT detect it; (2) naloxone response is inconsistent and may require higher doses or repeated dosing; (3) it is circulating in pressed counterfeit pills mixed with fentanyl. Maintain high suspicion in any refractory opioid presentation.

Annals of Emergency Medicine
Medetomidine in the Illicit Drug Supply: EM Implications
Annals EM Toxicology

Lynch et al. (Annals EM, 2026) examine the growing presence of medetomidine — a veterinary alpha-2 agonist — in the illicit drug supply. Unlike xylazine (which responds to dexmedetomidine antagonism), medetomidine has no approved reversal agent and causes profound bradycardia and sedation. The authors review detection challenges, ED management principles (supportive care, atropine for bradycardia), and withdrawal considerations for patients using contaminated opioids.

Health Care Sharing Ministry Tax Parity Act Introduced in Congress
Tax Policy Federal

Reps. Mike Kelly (R-PA), Greg Murphy MD (R-NC), and Chris Smith (R-NJ) introduced the Health Care Sharing Ministry Tax Parity Act, which would classify HCSM monthly share payments as qualified medical expenses — making them tax-deductible under the same rules that govern insurance premiums. For HCSM members (estimated 1.3 million Americans sharing $1B+ annually), this would effectively provide the same pre-tax treatment that employer-sponsored insurance enjoys. The bill is in Ways & Means subcommittee. Companion to the IRS proposed HRA rule.
